About

A leader at the intersection of where businesses
and industries meet machine intelligence.

Born in Chile and forged in the world's most demanding and complex mining operations, I have spent 14 years translating the language of mining and processing ore bodies into the language of algorithms. I have led digital transformation at two of the planet's largest mining companies, Glencore and Codelco, driving complex innovation portfolios and value through AI, advanced analytics, autonomy, electrification, automation, and IT-OT convergence.

I hold an Australian Global Talent visa, recognition by the Australian Government of exceptional ability in the resources technology sector. My work sits at the rare intersection where engineering rigour meets machine learning possibility, where a decade of operational instinct informs every model I have built with my teams. I lead with curiosity, execute with precision, and believe the most important technology is still the people who use it.

None of what is written here was achieved alone. Throughout my career, I have been surrounded by highly skilled and capable people who taught me, challenged me, and were patient with my endless questions. I hope that I have also created a positive impact in their lives. They know who they are, and I thank each of them sincerely.

Expertise Model

The Comb-Shaped Professional

The comb-shaped professional combines broad cross-disciplinary knowledge with deep expertise across multiple domains, enabling them to lead at the intersection of complex systems. I value this framework not only as a personal development model but as a tool I have used throughout my career to model human ability and skillsets. When assembling high-performance teams, I treat people as pieces of a puzzle: I never bring individuals with similar shapes together, as that polarises thinking and produces biased outcomes. Instead, I deliberately combine different shapes to cover a bigger picture and maximise collective capability.

Philosophy

What I Stand For

◆

Excellence

High standards are not negotiable, and they are what everyone should always strive for. Every deliverable, every decision, every interaction, executed with the same rigour I would apply to a production-critical system.

◆

Loyalty

I invest in people and partnerships for the long term. Trust is built through consistency, not contracts. The teams I have led know I have their back.

◆

Growth

Stagnation is the only real failure. I pursue continuous improvement relentlessly: in the models I build, the teams I lead, and the person I am becoming.

◆

Knowledge

Rigour over shortcuts, and a balance between applied insights and theoretical abstraction. I have learned to ask better questions by spending years at the front, doing and optimising, and to value everyone's knowledge and experience, even over the simplest things.

◆

Balance

Sustainable performance requires sustainable living. The same systems thinking I apply to mining operations, I apply to how I work and live. All of us need to "sharpen the saw" from time to time.
